## What Makes "Boring" UI Work

### 1. **Diegetic Design**
UI elements exist in the game world, not just for player convenience.

**Good examples:**
- Papers Please: Stamps, rule book, desk layout
- Obra Dinn: Insurance notebook, pocket watch
- Alt+F+This: **Terminal, task tracker, corporate dashboard**

**Bad examples:**
- Fantasy RPG with minimalist sans-serif UI (breaks immersion)
- Sci-fi game with wooden textures (doesn't match theme)

---

### 2. **Constraint as Strength**
Limitations force focus on what matters.

**Universal Paperclips:**
- No fancy graphics â†’ All focus on decision-making
- Plain text â†’ Numbers and choices are the game

**Papers Please:**
- Cramped desk â†’ Feel the claustrophobia
- Limited time â†’ Pressure is mechanical, not just narrative

**Alt+F+This:**
- Terminal aesthetic â†’ Feels like real work environment
- Text-heavy â†’ Relatable to devs (we live in terminals/slack)

---

### 3. **The Banality IS the Point**
When "boring" UI reflects boring work, it becomes satirical.

**Universal Paperclips:**
- Making paperclips SHOULD be boring â†’ That's why it's funny
- The mundanity of optimization â†’ Horror of optimization

**Alt+F+This opportunity:**
- Corporate dashboards ARE boring â†’ That's the satire
- Jira/Slack/Terminal aesthetic â†’ Relatable dread
- "Boring" = authentic suffering

---

### 4. **Readability Above All**
"Boring" doesn't mean unclear. Functional minimalism.

**Good boring:**
- Clear typography
- Strong hierarchy
- Obvious actions
- No visual noise

**Bad boring:**
- Ugly fonts
- Poor contrast
- Confusing layout
- Trying too hard to be "retro"
